What's a growth mindset?

    Kids with a growth mindset believe that intelligence can be developed. These students focus on learning over just looking smart, see effort as the key to success, and thrive in the face of a challenge. They know how to embrace the word 'yet'.

Frequently asked questions

Whats a fixed mindset?

People with a fixed mindset
- believe that intelligence and ability are born, static traits
- believe that they can do very little to change their ability
- view struggle as negative
- avoid challenges

What age group is this book for?

We recommend the book for kids who are 6 to 8 years old.

What is a growth mindset?

People with a growth mindset
- see that working hard and struggling pays off
- believe that success is a result of hard work
- understand that everyone can grow and learn
- view struggle as an opportunity to learn

About the author

When Esther was about 7 years old she had a phase during which she would’t do any of her homework alone. She thought couldn’t do it. And sometimes everybody even adults have this voice in my head that tells them that I can’t do something.

Esther recently went to a coding bootcamp. This experience was the hardest and most challenging thing she has ever done. And that’s where she learned about the growth mindset and the powerful word YET can be. It’s such a simple concept but important concept.

Now she would like for every child and their parents to embrace the growth mindset. That's doesnt mean that you are telling our kids that they can become anything. It’s only fair to tell them, they can become nearly anything, if they work hard and put a lot of effort into what they do.

Let's go from I can't do that to I can't do that yet.

Benefits for your kid

What a growth mindset can do for you and your child

Never Feel Stupid When Learning

Growth mindset allows your kid to realize that in order to be really good in something they have to put in the effort and that mistakes are there to learn from.

Enjoy Life, Even When You’re Not Good At It

“This is a wonderful feature of the growth mindset. You don’t have to think you’re already great at something to want to do it and to enjoy doing it.” – Dweck

Having Supportive Relationships

Kids with a growth mindset want to see their friends faults and help them to work on them, challenge them to become a better person, and encourage them to learn new things. This leads to healthier and happier relationships

Strengthens Confidence

With a growth mindset you don’t care about proving yourself to others, you only care about improving and growing. Your kid knows that there is no need to be perfect all the time.

Lowers Risk of Depression

In a study performed by Baer, Grant, and Dweck (2005), students in the fixed mindset had higher levels of depression because they ruminated over problems and setbacks

Be Better at Taking Responsibility For Their Life

A fixed mindset tries to repair self-esteem after a failure by assigning blame and making excuses. A growth mindset helps you take responsibility for your actions and decisions because you don’t blame others for them.
